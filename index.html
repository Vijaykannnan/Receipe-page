Classic Cheesecake Recipe

Look no further for a cream and ultra smooth classic Cheesecake recipe! Paired with a buttery graham cracker crust, no one can deny its simple decandence. For the best result, bake in a water bath
  • YIELDS

    • 12 servings

    PREP TIME
    45 minutes
    COOK TIME
    1 hour
    TOTAL TIME
    7,75 hours

    Ingredients

    Graham Cracker Crust

    1 and 1/2 cups (150g) graham cracker crumbs (about 10 full sheet graham crackers)

    5 Tablespoons (70g) unsalted butter, melted

    1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar

    Cheesecake
    four 8-ounce blocks (904g) full-fat cream cheese, softened to room temperature

    1 cup (200g) granulated sugar

    1 cup (240g) full-fat sour cream,at room temperature

    1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

    2 teaspoons fresh lemon juice(optional,but recommended)

    3 large eggs,at room temperature

    topping suggestion:salted caramel,lemon curd,strawberry topping,chocolate ganache,red wine chocalate ganache, freah fruit,whipped cream,or raspberry sauce(recipe in notes)

    Instructions

    1The oven rack to the lower-middle position and preheat oven to 350°F (177°C).


    2Make the crust :Using a food processor, pulse the graham crackers into crumbs. Pour into a medium sbowl and stir in sugar and melted butter untill combined.(You can also pluse it all together in the food processor.) Mixture will be sandy. Press firmly into the bottom and slightly up the sides od a 9-inch or 10 inch springform pan. No no need to grease the pan first.I use the bottom of a mesuring cup to pock the crust down tight.Pre-break for 8 minutes.Remove from the oven and place the hot pan for the water bath in step 4.Allows crust to slight cools as you prepare the filling.

    3Make the filling:Using a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the cream cheese and granulated sugar together on mediun-high speed in a large bowl until the mixture is smooth and cream ,about 2 minutes .Add the sour cream, vanilla extract, and lemon juice then beat until fully combined .On medium speen, add the egg once at athime , beating after each addition until just blended .After the final egg is incorporated into the batter ,stop mixing. To help prevent the cheesecakes from deflating and cracxking as it cools, avoid over-mixing the batter as best you can.

    4prepare the simple water bath (see note)Boil the pot of water You need 1 inch of water in your rosting pan for the water bath so make sure you boil enough, I use an kettle of hot water As the water is heating up, wrap the aluminium foil around the springform pan.Pour the cheesecake batter on the top of the crust. Use a rubber spatula or spoon to smooth in into an even layer . Place the pan inside of the pan and place in the oven first, then pour the hot water in.Whichever is easier for you.

    5Bake cheese cake for 55-70 minutes 0r untill the center is almost set.When it's done, the center of the cheesecake will slightely wobble if you gently shake the pan. Turn the oven off and open the oven door slightly .let the cheesecake sit in the oven in the water bath as it cools down for 1 hour.Remove from the oven and water bath,then cool cheesecake completely at room temperature .Then refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight

    6Use aknife to loosen a chilled cheese cake from the rim of the springform pan, then remove the rim.Using a clean sharp knife,cut into slices,wipe the knife clean and dip into warm water between each slice

    7Serve cheesecake with desired toppings,Cover and store leftover cheesecake in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
